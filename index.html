The Original Starbucks Coffee - Seattle, WA

The original Starbucks coffee! located in Pike Place Market, Seattle WA. This was where Starbucks opened their first store in 1971, offering fresh roasted coffee, tea, and spices from around the world! The name was inspired by the novel Moby-Dick. Since then, Starbucks has opened store locations around the world for millions of customers and has become part of thousands of neighborhoods everywhere!

The Seattle Great Wheel - Pier 57 - Seattle, WA

The Seattle Great Wheel is a 175 feet tall Ferris wheel at Pier 57 on Elliott Bay in Seattle WA. It was the tallest Ferris wheel in the United States at the time it opened in 2012. You can see a light show and have dinner while you ride the Great Wheel. The Great wheel was also feature in the adventure game Last Of Us Part II.

Fran's Chocolates - Downtown Seattle, WA

Fran's Chocolates were created by Fran Bigelow, A master chocolatier with an intense love for sublime chocolate. Fran's creations were inspired by a visit to Pairs, with exquisite ingredients. One of the most famous moments in Fran's Chocolate history is when president Obama mentioned Fran's "Smoke Salt Caramels" were his favorite chocolates. After that, the demand for the caramels was so high that Fran had to scale back the production of her other sweets just to keep up with the demand!

Space Needle

The Space Needle is considered the icon of Seattle and a designated landmark. It was built in the Seattle Center for the 1962 World's Fair. It was once the tallest structure west of the Mississippi river. It was built to withstand wind and earthquakes up to 9.0 magnitude. It has an Observation deck at the top of it. There is also a restaurant with a rotating see-through floor.

Dog in the Park - Downtown Seattle, WA

Dog in the Park is home of the original "Seattle Dog". So "What is a Seattle dog?" It is a grilled hotdog with cream cheese and grilled veggies! Established in 2005. It is a family owned business in the heart of Seattle. One of the best hotdogs you will ever have!

Piroshky Piroshky

Welcome to Piroshky Piroshky! It opened in 1992, and is located in Pike Place Market. Piroshky Piroshky brings a unique taste of Eastern European food to the Pacific Northwest. So what is a "piroshky"? It's an Eastern European turnover sandwich with local flavors locked inside! This is an experience like nothing else! You must try it!

Bainbridge Island Ferry

The Bainbridge Island Ferry. This is a ferry that you drive your car on, which then takes you across Puget Sound, to Bainbridge island, WA. It is a an 8.6 mile journey, where you can leave your car parked and make your way to the upper deck where a restaurant is. They have warm foods from the local Seattle restaurants and hot chocolate. You can take a seat at a table, or step outside for the amazing scenery. Once the ferry has stopped, you get back into your car and then continue your journey into Bainbridge island.

The Gum Wall

The Gum Wall is an alley way where brick walls ae covered in chewing gum. It is under Pike Place market, in downtown Seattle! Some people think its gross while other people find it entertaining. It is an considered an official tourist attraction in Seattle, and YES! you can add your own gum to the wall! The gum wall was unintentionally created in 1990.

Pike Place Market

Pike Place Market. This is the famous market in Seattle where they toss fish! It opened in 1907, and is one of the oldest farmers markets in the United states. It overlooks Elliott Bay waterfront on Puget Sound. It is full of farmers, craftspeople, and merchants. The market also features Rachel the Piggy Bank at the main entrance. Rachel the Piggy Bank was installed to raise money for social service agencies in the Market, and to help nurture the thriving Market community.

The Seattle Underground

The Seattle Underground is a network of passageways and basements in the Pioneer Square neighborhood of Seattle, WA. It is where the original city of Seattle was built. After the Great Seattle fire in 1889, construction was required to restore the city, and the streets were regraded one or two stories higher. The Underground tunnels still hold what is left of the original city.
